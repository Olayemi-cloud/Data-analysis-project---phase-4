Missing values in the primary outcome variable pain_score_final were present in both groups, with a higher proportion in the intervention group (13.6%) compared to the control group (7.1%). This pattern suggests that missingness may be differential by group, supporting the use of missing data diagnostics and appropriate handling strategies such as multiple imputation under the Missing At Random (MAR) assumption. A logistic regression model was fitted to assess the missingness mechanism of pain_score_final. The results showed that missingness was significantly associated with the treatment group (p < 0.001) and baseline pain score (p = 0.017), suggesting the missing data mechanism was consistent with Missing At Random (MAR). Thus, multiple imputation was used to address the missing values.

The results revealed a statistically significant difference between centres ((2) = 163.35, p < 0.001), suggesting variability in participant medication burden across sites. This finding justifies consideration of trial centre as a stratification factor or subgroup in further analyses, especially in light of concerns regarding polypharmacy in the target population.
